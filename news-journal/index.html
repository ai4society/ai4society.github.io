NewsLog
2024
February

Our group was involved at the premier AI conference, AAAI 2024, with:

  • A tutorial on 'Harnessing Large Language Models for Planning: A Lab on Strategies for Success and Mitigation of Pitfalls during that time' on Feb 21st.
  • A paper called 'From Cloud to Edge: Rethinking Generative AI for Low-Resource Design Challenges' at 'Artificial Intelligence for Design Problems' bridge program on Feb 21st.
  • A paper on deployed application: 'Promoting Research Collaboration with Open Data Driven Team Recommendation in Response to Call for Proposals' on Feb 22nd. Presented with the Deployed Application Award.
  • A demonstration paper on 'Expressive and Flexible Simulation of Information Spread Strategies in Social Networks Using Planning' on Feb 24th. Presented with the Best Demo Award.
  • A workshop on 'AI for Credible Elections: A Call To Action with Trusted AI' on Feb 26th.

Our group has lead the University of South Carolina in getting itself included as a collaborator with the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) for the Artificial Intelligence Safety Institute Consortium (AISIC). [announcement, members]

Vishal Pallagani got elected to the Student Committee of the AAAI - the international Association for the Advancement of Artificial Intelligence!

Likitha Valluru successfully defended her dissertation proposal on February 2nd: Trustable Methods for Group Recommendations With Application to Team Formation.
